My name is John Holopainen Hadaway (b. 1998). I am a programmer and writer, primarily interested in facilitating unique, resilient patterns of collaboration and coexistence. In my life and work, I aim to make things that give us agency both in representation and engagement, that help us pool and allocate resources and knowledge towards common goals, and that make it easier for us to cooperate across diversity.

At the moment, I am exploring. I am making _ourchive, helping develop A Real Space in the heart of Helsinki, and working with SPIN Unit. I am also learning about social and civic technology, community infrastructure, organisational design, and cities.

Previously, I studied at the University of Chicago and the Architectural Association (sort of). Professionally, I analysed and visualised spatial data for local and regional governments with SPIN Unit, researched experiences of democracy at the University of Chicago, worked on ad hoc design tasks at the HKU Guild, researched information technology policy at HKUST, built websites, wrote some code, and did non-profit and strategy consulting, among other things.

Before that, I was born into a rootless, nomadic childhood, lovingly unsettled in Jakarta, Manila, Cebu City, Moscow, and Almaty. I have since lived in Chicago, London, Montpellier, and Helsinki, where I am currently based. I am from Australia, Finland, and the Philippines.

If any of the above interests you, I would love to get in touch for any (or no) reason.
