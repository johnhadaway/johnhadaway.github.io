- [This article](https://www.technologyreview.com/2023/02/09/1067821/design-thinking-retrospective-what-went-wrong/) explores the [rise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Empire) and [fall](https://www.nytimes.com/2023/09/15/style/roman-empire-men-tiktok-instagram.html), as it were, of design thinking.
- [This blog post](https://jzhao.xyz/posts/agentic-computing) is a deeply inspiring and hopeful reflection on a possible direction for the internet.

- [A series of claims](https://aviv.medium.com/when-we-change-the-efficiency-of-knowledge-operations-we-change-the-shape-of-society-d48ca870ff5b) on the transformations that could arise from AI-driven enhancements in the efficency of knowledge operations
- [This article](https://www.noemamag.com/democracys-missing-link/) on the idea of a "preferendum," a method allowing citizens to rate and rank policy ideas. A richer interface for public opinion?
- [An argument](https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2019/07/more-on-street-by-street-zoning.html) for experimenting with street-by-street zoning in "NIMBY-dysfunctional areas," reasoning, as I understood it, that by reducing the scale of zoning decisions down to individual streets, communities might be more open to modest development projects. 
- [A working paper](https://cip.org/research/generative-ai-digital-commons) delves into the governance models for Generative Foundation Models, highlighting the need for better data rights frameworks and proposing transparency measures and shared ownership structures. 
- [This article](https://worksinprogress.co/issue/markets-in-fact-checking) explores the potential of prediction markets as a tool to expose and combat online misinformation. 
- [This review](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/your-book-review-progress-and-poverty) of Henry George's work, "Progress & Poverty," exploring its critique of 19th-century economic paradigms and its relevance to modern-day issues like housing affordability and income inequality. 
- [This article](https://worksinprogress.co/issue/building-back-faster) on the bureaucratic barriers hindering renewable energy projects.
- A thought-provoking [taxonomy of social networks](https://newpublic.substack.com/p/e2708a8a-4218-4158-a95d-21d9d218a2f7).
- [This article](https://mattsclancy.substack.com/p/when-technology-goes-bad?utm_source=substack&utm_medium=email) discusses the potential perils of technological advancement, particularly AI, in enabling malicious uses.
