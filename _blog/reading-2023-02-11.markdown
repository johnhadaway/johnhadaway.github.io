I figured "some things that I have been reading (and bobbing my head to)" would be a better title for this specific post than the plain, traditional "some things I have been reading" -- and yes, a corpus of two posts is a "tradition." I have been listening to a lot of music lately, and I would like to record that unimportant fact here, for posterity and all that.

- [This research](https://otherinter.net/research/local-gov/) explores the role of neighborhood associations in local governance as digital technologies reshape civic participation.
- [This idea](http://booktwo.org/notebook/the-tumblr-museum/) for a "minimum viable museum" is pretty neat.
- [This project](https://www.simonweckert.com/googlemapshacks.html), also pretty neat, hijacks Google Maps to create a "virtual traffic jam" by wheeling a bunch of second-hand smartphones around a city on a children's toy wagon.
- [This article](https://www.wired.com/story/50-years-later-we-still-dont-grasp-the-mother-of-all-demos/) takes a look at the 1968 presentation by Doug Engelbart of the "oN-Line System", the first hypertext system, and Englebart's vision for computing more generally.
- To my taste, these records stood out: [New Energy](https://fourtet.bandcamp.com/album/new-energy) by Four Tet, [Geogaddi](https://boardsofcanada.bandcamp.com/album/geogaddi) by Boards of Canada, [Our Love](https://caribouband.bandcamp.com/album/our-love) by Caribou, and [We're New Here](https://jamiexx.bandcamp.com/album/were-new-here) by Gil Scott-Heron and Jamie xx.
- [This green paper](https://www.theatlantic.com/technology/archive/2018/02/what-is-the-internet/553000/) presents a theoretical basis and technical sketch for a pluralistic social network. The theoretical basis and the design parameterisation are both really thought-provoking.
- [This new organisation](https://cip.org/whitepaper), CIP, presents a compelling vision for the governance of transformative technologies.
- [This review](https://crookedtimber.org/2022/09/07/the-democratic-theory-of-a-half-built-garden/) of Ruthanna Emrys' _A Half-Built Garden_ made me incredibly excited to read it.
- I finally got around to reading [this paper](https://www2.econ.iastate.edu/tesfatsi/ArchitectureOfComplexity.HSimon1962.pdf) by Herbert Simon, "The Architecture of Complexity." It is dense and difficult, at least for me, and I will certainly have to revisit it a few more times to fully wrap my head around it, but I think it's worth the effort.
- [This article](https://knightcolumbia.org/content/protocols-not-platforms-a-technological-approach-to-free-speech) argues "that we return to a world of protocols dominating the internet, rather than platforms" and does so quite convincingly.
- [This article](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2019/07/interoperability-fix-internet-not-tech-companies) provided much-needed nuance to my understanding of the concept of "interoperability" and the role it plays in the current debate over the future of the internet.
